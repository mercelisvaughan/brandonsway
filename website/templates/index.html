Journey To Brandon's Way.

Brandon Wells, an East Baltimore native, encountered many challenges growing up in the Belair Edison neighborhood until he met his mentor Mr. Tony James. Mr. James saw something in Brandon that made him believe that he could change and provided steadfast support that opened Brandon's mind to other possibilities. This ultimately led him to overcoming drug affliations, violence, and peer pressure leaving Brandon with an immense obligation, duty, and commitment to influence that type of change in others.

Brandon understands that true and lasting change occurs over time; it is not immediate, instantaneous or linear. Moreover, long lasting change and delayed gratification have a major impact on social emotional health and directly affect overall life success.

In 2009, Brandon became a Special Education Para Professional with Baltimore City Public Schools. Capitalizing on his unique gift to encourage, influence, and motivate youth, he felt confined by the limits of the school environment and desired to do more. This led him to becoming a Psychiatric Rehabilitation Program(PRP) counselor with Helping Hands Services. Since that time, he has serviced numerous families inclusive of children, adolescents, and adults.

Brandon credits his ability to form, foster, and maintain healthy relationships with clients as a major attribute to his PRP success. Enlarging the span of his influence, in winter 2019, Brandon partnered with Helping Hands Services, and founded an affliate PRP organization, Brandon's Way.

Ways We Help

Psychiatric Rehabilitation Program

Our PRP assists youth and adults, who have a qualifying mental illness by offering community-based and individual services that enhance skills related to daily living.

Therapy Behavioral Services

This form of therapy looks to identify and help change potentially self-destructive or unhealthy behaviors. It's derived from the notion that all behaviors are learned and that behaviors can be changed.

Mental Health Services

We help build the emotional, psychological, and social well-being of our clients.

Supported Employment Program

Our SEP program helps integrate individuals with disabilities into competitive work environments.

Mission

Brandon's Way desires to be an example of hope and integrity for our clients to achieve their maximum potential while overcoming mental health disorders, trauma, and negative societal influences.

We offer individual and group counseling services that develop the following.

  • Increased Self Esteem.
  • Heightened Self Awareness.
  • Conflict Resolution Skills.
  • Positive Relationships.
